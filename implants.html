Bone Grafting & Sinus Lifts

Bone Grafting

Bone loss and shrinkage is an inevitable consequence of tooth loss. The amount and extent of bone loss depends upon several factors including technique of extraction, time since extraction, wearing a denture following extraction and patients genetic susceptibility.

Bone grafting procedures may sometimes be indicated either, at the time of implant placement or at a separate appointment before implant placement to build the bone back to successfully host the implant.

At Artisan Implants we use a host of bone grafting materials and procedures, ranging from small particulate bone graft materials for smaller defects to Block bone grafts for more significant bone defects.

Bone graft materials can be derived from your own bone or harvested from a human donor. These can also be sourced from cattle or can be synthetic in origin. Regardless of their origin all bone graft materials act as a scaffold for your new bone to form.

Sinus Lifts

This procedure, also called Sinus Augmentation, is required when there is insufficient bone in the back of the upper jaw to successfully place dental implants.
There are 2 ways to carry out the procedure:

1.Crestal Approach: This is employed when there is some bone height present but not enough to house the entire implant length, rotary or manual instruments are used to push the sinus floor and membrane up followed by placement of grafting material and implant at the same time.

2.Lateral Window Sinus Grafts: This approach is used when there is a very significant vertical bone height deficiency. In this method a precise window is prepared in the sinus bone wall at the site of the implant placement to gain access to place bone graft in the sinus. During your consultation we will explain whether you require a sinus lift.

What is a Specialist Prosthodontist?

A specialist prosthodontist treats very complex dental matters. This includes the restoration and replacement of missing or damaged teeth.

They receive extensive training in dental implants, crowns, bridges, dentures, jaw disorders, and more. As a result, prosthodontists are highly skilled in their field and can provide patients with the best possible care.

Prosthodontists are best placed providing full mouth rehabilitation on Implants using techniques such as all on 4/6, teeth in a day as well as use of zygomatic implants.

Your Dental Implant Journey at Dental Excellence

1. Assessment and Planning:

At this appointment, the dentist will discuss all possible alternatives and will assess suitability for dental implant provision. Along with other assessments, X-rays and 3D bone imaging will be done, if required. A written treatment plan will follow detailing the cost and sequence of treatments.

2. Implant Placement:

Implant placement is a simple minor surgical procedure that is performed under sterile conditions. Bone regeneration, if required, can be done at this stage or sometimes prior to this depending upon the individual requirements.

3. Integration or Rest Period:

Implants can typically take two to six months to fuse with the bone. During this time temporary teeth, which can be removable dentures or fixed teeth on implants or adjacent teeth can be worn as appropriate.

4. Restoration:

Once the bone has fused with the implants, the final restoration can be made as planned, ranging from a single fixed crown, small or full mouth fixed bridge or a removable overdenture.
